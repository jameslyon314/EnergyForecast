Instructions: Using the controls underneath each graph, try to enact a plan of how you would like each energy source to develop in each sector (i.e. try to eliminate fossil fuels), while maintaining the expected energy requirements for the entire time(from EIA.gov). First slider is the amount of power that the source will contribute (0 to 4000 TWh), the second is the method by which it will reach its end goal (exponentially (fast start), quadratically (fast start), linearly, quadratically (slow start), exponentially (slow start), or unchanged from 2018 levels). Tick the checkbox if you want a certain source to reach its goal by a specific year, controlled by the neighboring slider. Note, that increasing the electricity demand in the Residential/Commercial, Industrial, and Transportation sectors will increase the goal line in the Generation sector. You may notice renewables/noncarbon sources have carbon emissions in the bottom tier or graphs, this is because lifecycle emissions are considered here (i.e. carbon emissions needed to mine ores, set concrete, etc.)
